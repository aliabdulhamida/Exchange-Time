Understanding the Fear & Greed Index

The Fear & Greed Index gauges investor sentiment by analyzing various market indicators. Extreme fear often signals potential buying opportunities when investors are overly cautious, while extreme greed may indicate market peaks when complacency sets in.

Disclaimer

This information is provided for educational purposes only and should not be considered investment advice.

Index Components

  • Stock Price Strength: Measures the number of stocks hitting 52-week highs versus lows, indicating market confidence.
  • Stock Price Breadth: Tracks the volume of advancing stocks compared to declining stocks, providing insight into market participation.
  • Market Volatility: Analyzes the VIX (Volatility Index) to gauge fear levels and potential market instability.
  • Put and Call Options: Examines the ratio of put options to call options for sentiment analysis, reflecting investor expectations.
  • Safe Haven Demand: Compares returns on stocks versus bonds to assess risk appetite in the market.
  • Junk Bond Demand: Evaluates the spread between junk bond yields and safer investments, highlighting investor risk tolerance.
  • Market Momentum: Tracks the S&P 500 relative to its 125-day moving average, indicating overall market direction.
